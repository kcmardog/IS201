The Girl Who Drank the Moon by Kelly Barnhill

  1. Writing Style
    • This book has a very unique writing style, and that's one of the reasons it's in my top 3. It has very short sentences on average that give it a dramatic tone. I find the style very compelling.
  2. Re-Read Value
    • Given that I have read this book 3 times already, I would say it has a pretty high re-read value. It takes a lot to get me to re-read or re-watch anything, so this book impressed me.
  3. Overall Enjoyment
    • Without spoiling anything, this book has a lot of complex themes in it that can be related to the real world. I find the various characters interesting and unique, and they each develop significantly throughout the book.

The Giver by Lois Lowry

  1. Writing Style
    • This book is beautifully written. It is captivating and makes you not want to stop reading it. This book differs from The Girl Who Drank the Moon because it does not stand out for its writing style. However, the writing works quiety in the background to create a literary masterpiece.
  2. Re-Read Value
    • This is another book that I have re-read a few times. Even when you know how the plot will end, it is still such an interesting and thought-provoking read, especially because it follows a dystopian theme.
  3. Overall Enjoyment
    • The Giver is very enjoyable, not because it is a "feel good" book, but because it makes you think about society. I will note that the books that follow The Giver in its series are not nearly as good. The first book certainly shines.

The Importance of Being Earnest by Oscar Wilde

  1. Writing Style
    • This book, admittedly, is not a book. It is a play, meaning that the writing style is purely dialogue accompanied by some scene and action descriptions. While not as descriptive as other books due to the fact that it is only dialogue, the speech itself is very entertaining.
  2. Re-Read Value
    • I have also read this book several times, which is easy because of its short length. The extreme dry humor in this book never stops making me laugh out loud as I read it. I can recognize that this kind of humor does not appeal to everyone, but for me it is a classic humorous read.
  3. Overall Enjoyment
    • As aforementioned, I find this book extremely enjoyable due to the humor employed. It is short, quick, witty, and hilarious all in one. Sometimes, I open to a random page and just start reading to lighten my mood.
